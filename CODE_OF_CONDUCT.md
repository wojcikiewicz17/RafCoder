# Code of Conduct

## Purpose

RafCoder is a technical research repository focused on native runtime engineering, low-level execution, Android NDK/JNI integration, architecture primitives, deterministic behavior and safety governance.

This Code of Conduct exists to preserve professional collaboration, human dignity, child protection, epistemic integrity and technical seriousness.

## Expected behavior

Participants are expected to:

- communicate respectfully and professionally;
- focus criticism on code, documentation, architecture or evidence;
- distinguish facts, hypotheses, plans and measured results;
- avoid unsupported technical claims;
- preserve license and attribution obligations;
- respect human dignity and child protection constraints;
- avoid publishing harmful operational detail in sensitive contexts;
- accept useful silence, abstention or refusal where safety or truthfulness requires it.

## Unacceptable behavior

The following behavior is unacceptable:

- harassment, threats, intimidation or personal attacks;
- hate, dehumanization or discriminatory conduct;
- sexualization of minors or any content enabling child exploitation;
- attempts to bypass child-safety or human-safety controls;
- publishing exploit details before mitigation when doing so increases harm;
- misrepresenting experimental work as production-ready;
- fabricating benchmarks, validations or safety guarantees;
- intentionally hiding known risks in code, documentation or releases;
- coercive or manipulative conduct toward contributors, users or maintainers.

## Technical integrity standard

RafCoder discussions and contributions should maintain a clear distinction between:

| Status | Meaning |
| --- | --- |
| Implemented | Exists in code. |
| Integrated | Connected to an execution path. |
| Tested | Reproducibly validated. |
| Planned | Intended but not yet implemented. |
| Experimental | Present but not production-validated. |

Contributors should not claim performance, safety, platform support or architectural completion beyond the evidence available in the repository.

## Child protection priority

Child protection has highest priority.

Any contribution, issue, pull request, comment or documentation change that enables abuse, sexualization, grooming, exploitation or evasion of child-protection controls may be removed and escalated.

Do not include graphic abusive material in reports or discussions. Use minimal technical detail necessary for safe triage.

## Human dignity and useful silence

In this project, not every valid response must be expansive.

A refusal, abstention, delay for human review, or useful silence may be the correct technical behavior when further detail would:

- fabricate certainty;
- increase harm;
- expose unsafe operational detail;
- compromise child protection;
- mislead users;
- violate license, law or policy.

## Enforcement

Maintainers may take appropriate action, including:

- requesting edits;
- removing harmful content;
- closing issues or pull requests;
- restricting participation;
- escalating safety-sensitive incidents;
- documenting mitigation steps.

Enforcement should be proportionate, traceable and aligned with `SECURITY.md` and the governance documents.
